# Template: Release / news post (format-neutral)

Describes the *structure and voice* of a release or news post. The exact layout
and the rendering (shortcodes/components) come from the repo's writing-profile.

## Shape

- **Key takeaways (lead)** — open with 2–3 short, scannable takeaways: the most
  important thing, the main player benefit, the scope/context of the update.
- **Hook (1–2 sentences)** — why this matters to the player, in benefit terms,
  not version numbers. ("Faster hand-history sync" beats "v2.4.0 released").
- **What changed** — the concrete changes. When announcing **features**, give each
  one its own short section: what it is, the problem it solves, the concrete
  benefit for the player. Group as *New / Improved / Fixed* when it's a list of
  smaller changes instead.
- **Why it matters** — pull the changes together into the payoff in the player's
  day-to-day; a short bulleted recap works well.
- **CTA** — one clear next action (download, try it, read the guide). See the
  brand doc's CTA library for canonical wording.

## Voice

- Energetic but honest; no overpromising. Lead with benefit, name the feature
  second.
- Credit fixes plainly; don't bury or spin known issues that were resolved.
- Concrete and practical; speak to the player's goal, not their rank.

## Length

- A feature-announcement news post runs longer (per-feature sections); a small
  maintenance post is just a hook + a bulleted list + a CTA. Serve the objective
  over a fixed word count — see the repo profile for the target length.
